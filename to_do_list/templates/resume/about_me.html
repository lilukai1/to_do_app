Andrea Wiley

Software developer, data lover, general nerd.



Hi There!

I have always been interested in Tech and Web Development. However, there just never seemed to be the right path for me to make it a career. When I was in high school, I programmed and played on a MUDD. If you've never played a MUDD, they came around at the beginning of the 56 k/b modems. Using Telnet, you could log into a UNIX server and play all kinds of role-playing, strategy, turn-based, or real-time text-based games. This is where I got my first exposure to C, C++, and command-line interfaces. I programmed a LOT for this game and its website.

When I started college, I got too busy for the game and eventually stopped playing. At college, I was unsure of what to major in. I tried engineering, English literature, biology, and had a host of other shorter-term ideas. Computers and tech were always in the back of my mind, but back then there were not a lot of women in tech. I remember going to the Comp Sci department at the University of Kansas when I was considering changing majors, and there was not a single woman. And to be honest, I had imposter syndrome even then. To be on my own, continually proving that as a woman I could hold my own against the horde of super-smart comp-sci majors was extremely intimidating. Eventually, I went into business school where the marketing emphasis degree combined psychology and data manipulation via statistics. After graduating, it helped me get a few jobs but none that were anywhere close to what I had envisioned. So I kept reading and kept growing and learning.

My next major experience with tech was a few years ago. I was heavily into stock trading and picked up some Java and Javascript to make some models. That was fun and educational, but I never developed the fortitude for day trading. I couldn't think of another project I was passionate about to use Java in, either. So again, I moved on.

This brings us to 2020. I live in a rural area, a very conservative part of the country. There is a deep distrust here of the media and the government. Since I've always liked programming and playing with large blocks of data, I started looking for graphs and charts to explain what we were seeing. This led me to Python. And boy, do I LOVE Python. I can think of a million projects I want to get done. I quickly realized I want to do this for LIFE.

I want to use my love of math and charting to help people understand complex topics.
I want to be a Python Programmer, and in the future, a data scientist.
